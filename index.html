I am a motivated professional in machine learning (ML) and artificial intelligence (AI) engineering with a strong background in deep learning, signal processing, and computer vision. My experience spans both theory and applications, from designing and implementing innovative ML algorithms to building software solutions for scientific data analysis. I have contributed to NSF-funded research projects and authored high-impact journal and conference papers in venues such as CVPR, ICCV, TMLR, IEEE TSP, NIMA, and ECML. My work has also led to multiple patents in the field of adversarial machine learning.

I enjoy designing end-to-end AI solutions, including data collection and cleaning, predictive model design, APIs, and user-facing tools, especially for high-dimensional and scientific data. Currently, I work on machine-learning-assisted radiation detector pulse analysis at the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB), including peak fitting, classification, and integration of ML into data acquisition (DAQ) workflows.
